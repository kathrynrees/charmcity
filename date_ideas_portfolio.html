Explore a new neighborhood

On Saturdays, check out the Victorian architecture of the Painted Ladies (pictured) in Abell and enjoy a breakfast biscuit from Blacksauce Kitchen at the 32nd St Farmers Market. If you're lucky, The Book Thing will be open for you to browse before heading to Peabody Heights for happy hour.


Walk around Inner Harbor

When the weather's nice, follow the walking path around Inner Harbor. Inner Harbor itself can get congested with tourists, but if you start the path in Fed Hill (along Key Highway), it's nice to see the water from different angles and to walk towards the National Aquarium.

Visit Mr. Trash Wheel

If you follow the walking path around Inner Harbor, you'll eventually pass Mr. Trash Wheel (pictured) — Baltimore's solar and hydro-powered trash collector.


Watch the Orioles play at Camden Yards

The Orioles' track record speaks for itself, but Camden Yards is a beautiful historic baseball stadium. Unlike stadiums in other cities, Camden Yards' ticket prices remain relatively low — and I'm not sure you'll find crab mac n cheese hot dogs anywhere else.

Enjoy the food scene

Baltimore ranked among Yelp's top 10 cities for food lovers in 2019. From Ekiben bao buns to Diablo Donuts (pictured), there are tons of delicious cafes, restaurants and pop-up stands to explore. Water for Chocolate and Clavel are among my favorites.


Picnic in a park

Located only a few blocks from the water near Fells Point, Canton and Highlandtown, Patterson Park has lots of places to picnic. In the spring, the park's makeshift mud pit is a fun place to watch Baltimore's annual Kinetic Sculpture Race. Check out more Baltimore parks to explore here.
