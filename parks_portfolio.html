Wyman Park

Stony Run creek and its walking trail run through this dog-friendly park. The park borders with Johns Hopkins University's undergrad campus as well as the neighborhoods of Hampden, Keswick and Remington. Among the shops and restaurants to explore nearby, check out Greedy Reads and Golden West Cafe.

Druid Hill Park

If you love plants, check out the Rawlings Conservatory (pictured) and its orchid room — or the weekly farmers' market. Walk around Druid Lake or play disc golf through the park's public course. Within the park, the Maryland Zoo is great for young kids. Walking distance from the park, refuel at Dovecote Cafe.

Sherwood Gardens

Every year, this park plants 50,000+ tulip bulbs. During peak bloom in late April/early May, you'll find lots of visitors picnicking or taking photos here. The gardens are located in Guilford, a neighborhood in northern Baltimore. This private park is open to the public so there are no tickets or admission fees required.

Middle Branch Park

Located in Cherry Hill, South Baltimore, Middle Branch Park looks onto the Patapsco River. In the spring, there's usually a cherry blossom celebration when the trees are in bloom. In the summer, you can rent canoes and kayaks or sign-up for a Full Moon Paddle through Baltimore Parks & Rec (select "Outdoor Activities" to make reservations). For nearby food with a view, Nick's Fish House looks onto the water (though I recommend it for the view more than the food).

Riverside Park

Located in Federal Hill, South Baltimore, this dog-friendly park is walking distance from Fed Hill's restaurants and bars. Pick up lunch at Cross Street Market — one of Baltimore's six remaining historic public markets — and head to Riverside Park for a picnic. Afterwards, check out The Brewer's Cask for happy hour or keep walking to Fed Hill Park for a view of Inner Harbor. If you head to Fed Hill, walking around the American Visionary Art Museum (at least the outside of it) is a must.

Farring-Baybrook Park

Located in Brooklyn, South Baltimore, Baybrook Park has tons of greenspace that is home to wild deer and many (unofficial) soccer fields. Brooklyn straddles the border of Baltimore City and Anne Arundel County and the City doesn't make it easy for residents or visitors to get here via public transportation, but having spent so much time at this beautiful park, I had to include it among my favorites. It also has a great view of the city skyline.
