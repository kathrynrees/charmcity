Assateague Island, MD

Located off Maryland's Eastern Shore, Assateague is known for its wild horses that roam the beach. As you drive to the island from Baltimore, you travel the Harriet Tubman Byway, which connects several historical sites, including the Harriet Tubman Underground Railroad National Historic Park.


Great Falls, VA

A short drive from DC, Great Falls National Park looks onto the Potomac River. This photo doesn't do it justice, but hiking along the rapids in autumn is beautiful.


Harpers Ferry, WV

Harpers Ferry has great canoing and hiking. The historic town lies at the intersection of the Potomac and Shenandoah Rivers. Here, you can stand in Maryland and look across the water at both Virginia and West Virginia.


Washington, D.C.

It's very easy to catch the MARC train from Penn Station to D.C. Only an hour away from Charm City, D.C. is especially nice to visit in the spring when the cherry blossoms are in bloom.
